Stemming

Stemming is NLP technique that lower inflection in words to their root forms
hence helps in the processing of text, words and coucments for text normalization.

Inflection is the process through which a words is modified to communicate many grammatical categories, including tense, case voice, aspect ,person, number, gender and mood.
thus althought a word may exist in several inflected forms, having multiple inflected forms inside the same text adds redundancy to the NLP process.
as result we emply stemming to reduce words to their basic form or stem, which may or may not be legitimate word in language.

types

1. port stemmer: PorterStemmer():
Porter Stemmer is the original stemmer and is renowned for its ease of use and rapidity.
Frequently the resultant stem in a shorter word with the same root meaning.
2. SnowBall stemmer: SnowballStemmer():
The method is utilized in this instance is more precise and is referred to "English stemmer" or "Porter2Stemmer"
It is somewhat faster and more logical than the orginal porter Stemmer
3. Lancaster stemmer: LancasterStemmer():
Produces results with excessive stemming, Overstemming renders stems non-linguistic or meaningless.
4. RegexpStemer (Regular Expression stemming algorithm):
With the help of this stemming algorithm, we can construct our own stemmer.
RegexpStemmer class with the help of which we can easily implement Regular Expression Stemmer algorithms. It basically takes a single regular expression and removes any prefix or suffix that matches the expression.
